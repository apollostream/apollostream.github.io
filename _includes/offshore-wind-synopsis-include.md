## The Historical Context: How We Got Here

To understand why this matters, you need to know a bit of history.

### The Old Way (1945-1972): Federal Dominance

When President Truman issued his famous proclamation in 1945 asserting federal control over the continental shelf, the federal government operated largely on its own. Washington made decisions about offshore resources, and states had little say.

### The New Way (1972-2025): Cooperative Federalism

That changed in 1972 with the Coastal Zone Management Act (CZMA). For the past 50+ years, federal decisions affecting state coastal areas have followed a cooperative model:

1. **Federal agencies consult with states** before major offshore actions
2. **The Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM)** runs task forces bringing together federal agencies, states, and stakeholders
3. **When conflicts arise** (like radar interference), they're resolved through project-specific mitigation—not blanket bans
4. **States have a voice** in federal consistency determinations

This cooperative approach has been used successfully for over a thousand federal offshore actions. It's how America balances federal authority with state interests.

The December 2025 pause abandoned all of this. No consultation. No task force meetings. No project-specific mitigation. Just a blanket suspension announced without warning.
